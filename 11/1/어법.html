Here’s an application of metaphors to research papers: Below-grade-level readers in my urban middle school have / has
a great deal of difficulty doing / to do
research. Their answer when assigned / assigning
to do “a report” is / are
to come to the media center and copy / copying
verbatim everything in the encyclopedia article or whatever / that
source they have located / have been located
, be it Internet, print, or whatever / whichever
. To try to get / getting
the students to understand / understand
why it / that
isn’t necessary / necessarily
to copy everything in the articles, I use a comparison to a supermarket. I ask them / themselves
if everything in the supermarket is / are
useful, and we discuss this, with the usual conclusion being / is
that it is, but we don’t buy everything all the time. Then I ask them why they don’t buy everything each time they go and how / that
they determine what / that
to buy when they go with their / its
parents. Someone usually mentions that they have a list. From that point on, I lead a discussion about how whatever their teachers have asked for is their “shopping list” for their research, and they should just read for that information and write it down in note format. It sometimes helps them to understand that copying everything is a wasteful endeavor.
